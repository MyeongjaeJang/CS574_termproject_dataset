Satyendra Pathak
Doha
The combined net profit of all 45 companies listed on the Qatar Stock Exchange (QSE) increased 1.1 percent to QR38.56 billion in 2017 from QR38.14 billion for the corresponding period in the previous year.
Vodafone Qatar's financial year changed from March 31 to December 31. Therefore, the financial information for the year ended December 31, 2017 represents only nine-month period.
The financial statements of banks listed on QSE showed a rise of 21.3 percent year-on-year in profits during the fourth quarter of 2017, according to statistics published by the Mubasher Qatar website.
Lenders' profits amounted to QR4.6 billion in the fourth quarter of 2017, up from QR3.8 billion in the same quarter a year earlier.
Profits of the nine banks levelled up 6.6 percent to QR21.03 billion in the fourth quarter of 2017 against QR19.73 billion in the same quarter of the previous year.
Qatar National Bank (QNB) generated an annual profit of QR13.1 billion, the highest among its peers.
Over the course of the last year, assets of the QSE-listed banks levelled up 9.6 percent to QR1.4 trillion against QR1.31 trillion in the previous year.
Qatari banks' operating revenues grew 3.7 percent to QR44.3 billion in the year ended December 2017 against QR42.7 billion in the previous year.
Cash dividends of the nine banks increased by 38.2 percent year-on-year to QR10.6 billion for 2017.
Qatar First Bank was the sector's only firm which didn't recommend distribution of cash dividends for the year.
The financial statements of the transportation companies listed on the QSE showed a surge of 88.8 percent year-on-year in profits during the fourth quarter of 2017, while their cash dividends recorded QR1.05 billion, according to Mubasher's statistics.
The transportation sector's profits increased to QR405.1 million in the fourth quarter of 2017, up from QR214.7 million in the same quarter a year earlier. By the end of 2017, assets of transportation firms declined 6.9 percent to QR48.7 million against QR52.4 million in the previous year.
Although the sector's annual performance declined, cash dividends of the three firms inched up 0.5 percent to QR1.05 billion in the fourth quarter of 2017 from QR1.04 billion in the same quarter of the previous year.
Profits of five insurance companies listed on QSE declined by 0.03 percent year-on-year during the fourth quarter of 2017, while their cash dividends amounted to QR711.2 million.
QSE-listed insurance firms posted QR402.8 million in profits in the fourth quarter of 2017, compared to QR402.9 million in the same period a year earlier.
By the end of 2017, assets of the Qatar-listed insurance companies levelled up 15.04 percent to QR47.29 billion against QR 41.11 billion in the previous year.
Cash dividends of the five insurance firms jumped 20.4 percent to QR711.23 million in 2017 compared to QR 590.79 million in the previous year.
