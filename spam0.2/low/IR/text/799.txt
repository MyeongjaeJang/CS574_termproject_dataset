Syria Puts in Action Humanitarian Corridor in Eastern Ghouta The Syrian government has regained control of the Eastern Ghouta, putting in action humanitarian corridor. Syrians continue leaving Eastern Ghouta via Muhayam al-Wafedin checkpoint through a humanitarian corridor in order to avoid victims amid an operation to liberate the territory from armed rebels. The Syrian government has regained control of the Eastern Ghouta, having put an end to years of rebel control amid a civil war and struggle against terrorists and put in action humanitarian corridor in Eastern Ghouta. The Syrian Army troops continued their mop-up operations in Zmelka region in Eastern Ghouta on Wednesday, discovering a large warehouse used by Faylaq al-Rahman terrorists for hoarding medicines and medical equipment.
