Makerere University suspends senior staff – Edward Kisuze for sexually harassing female student   
Makerere University has suspended Mr Edward Kisuze, a senior administrative assistant in the Academic Registrar’s Department for allegedly harassing a female student in his office. 
In a statement recorded at Makerere police, Njeri said that on April 13, 2018 she was sexually harassed by Kisuze in Room 507. The student said that Kisuze “grabbed my boobs, started kissing my thighs” and “put fingers in my vagina” from his office at Makerere University Senate building. - Advertisement - 
The student says the man kissed her private parts forcefully and in order for her to get evidence, she took a selfie as there was no camera in the room. A photo of the selfie has been shared on various media platforms Prof. William Bazeyo, the acting Vice Chancellor Makerere University –- after Kisuze failed to provide satisfactory response to defend himself, has decided to suspend him. 
In a letter dated April 17, 2018, which has also been posted on the university’s Twitter page, the Acting Vice Chancellor, Professor William Bazeyo said Kisuze had been suspended from university service on half pay with immediate effect to allow a smooth investigation into allegations that he harassed the student on Tuesday at his office at Senate Building. 
Prof Bazeyo’s letter to Kisuze says, “I hereby suspend you from university service on half pay with immediate effect to allow a smooth investigation into the matter. You are strongly advised to desist from any access to the senate building premises and interactions with students of Makerere University until investigations are completed.” 
According to the professor Bazeyo, a committee has been set up to investigate into the matter, and the accused has been asked to handover all university property that may be in your possession.” Makerere University has over the years come under public scrutiny over cases of sexual misconduct. - Advertisement -
