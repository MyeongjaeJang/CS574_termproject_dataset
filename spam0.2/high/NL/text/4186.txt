Huawei tops Spain phone ranking as sales fall - study Monday 26 March 2018 | 11:41 CET | News 
Huawei was the top-selling smartphone brand in Spain in February, a month that saw sales plummet 34.3 percent year on year, reports Xataka Movil, citing a study from market researcher Ideas Originales. The Chinese handset maker accounted for 26.3 percent of sales, ahead of Samsung with 24.5 percent, Apple with 11.4 percent, BQ with 7.0 percent and LG with 6.2 percent. ZTE, Alcatel and Xiaomi followed with 3.8 percent, 3.7 percent and 3.7 percent of the market respectively for total February unit sales of 792,000, said the report. 
In terms of value, Samsung, Apple and Huawei dominated the ranking with some 80 percent of the market, added the report. Samsung (30.2%) led the February figures, followed by Apple (29.8%) and Huawei (20.0%), with BQ (4.4%) and LG (4.0%) rounding out the top 5.
