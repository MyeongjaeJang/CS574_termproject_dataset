Accenture SA appoints Mngxati its new MD Monday 19 March 2018 | 13:37 CET | News 
Accenture has appointed Vukani Mngxati its country managing director for South Africa, effective 01 May, with responsibility for driving Accenture’s growth strategy in sub-Sahara Africa. Mngxati will succeed William Mzimba, who has been Accenture’s country managing director for South Africa since 2006 and with Accenture for eighteen years. 
Mngxati served as managing director of Accenture’s Health & Public Service business for the past five years. He started his career with Accenture in 2000 after completing his studies and left in 2006 to pursue his personal interests as an entrepreneur. He returned to Accenture four years. Accenture has been operating in South Africa since 1971. The company serves a wide range of clients in the country, from state-owned entities to private enterprises and multinationals, through its offices in Johannesburg, Cape Town and Pretoria.
