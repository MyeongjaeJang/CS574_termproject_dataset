Oppo unveils R15 wide-screen smartphone Tuesday 20 March 2018 | 13:33 CET | News 
Chinese smartphone maker Oppo has unveiled its new flagship smartphone, the R15. Available from 01 April, the device supports the latest features of a 19:9 display taking up nearly 90 percent of the 6.28-inch OLED screen and a notch at the top of the display for the camera and sensors. 
The R15 with glass body will be offered in a choice of hot red, snow white and starry purple, while a ceramic-body 'Dream' version comes in black or red. The basic version runs Mediatek's new Helio P60 processor, while the Dream model comes with Qualcomm's Snapdragon 660. Both feature 6 GB RAM and 128 GB storage, a microSD card slot for up to 256 GB external storage, Android 8.0 and a fingerprint scanner on the back. With a 3,400 mAh battery, they support Oppo's own Vooc fast-charging system. 
The cameras include 20 megapixels on the front as well as dual cameras on the back, of 16 megapixels and 5 or 20 megapixels depending on the model. The Dream model includes a f/1.7 aperture on both back lenses and a special face portrait mode.
