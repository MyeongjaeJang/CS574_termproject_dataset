Intel names new board member   16:24 CET | News Intel has elected Risa Lavizzo-Mourey to its board of directors. Lavizzo-Mourey in this way becomes the fifth new independent director added to the company’s board since the beginning of 2016. The board also voted to extend Andy Bryant’s term as Intel chairman. Bryant became Intel chairman in May 2012 and will stand for re-election at the company’s 2018 AGM. If elected, he will continue to serve as chairman until the 2019 AGM. 
Lavizzo-Mourey has served as the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation professor of Population Health and Health Equity at the University of Pennsylvania since January. From 2003 to 2017, she was the CEO of the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation, the largest US philanthropy organization dedicated to health. She is also a member of the boards of directors of General Electric and Hess.
