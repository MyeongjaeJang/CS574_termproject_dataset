Dodo, iPrimus, Commander to compensate customers for slow NBN speeds   11:33 CET | News The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC) has announced that Dodo, iPrimus and Commander have agreed to compensate their customers who were misled about maximum speeds they could achieve on certain NBN plans. Dodo, iPrimus and Commander have until 27 April to notify affected customers by email or letter. 
According to the ACCC, all other residential consumers or small businesses with speed issues relating to a service delivered over the NBN should first contact their service provider. If the complaint remains unresolved, the residential consumer or small business can make a complaint to the Telecommunications Industry Ombudsman online or by phone.
