Huawei's smartphone market share rises in South Africa  11 CET | News 
Huawei says it has made strides in the premium smartphone segment in South Africa, taking a 16.3 percent market share locally for all smartphone devices, and a 13.8 percent share for premium devices (USD 600), reports BusinessTech. A previous Effective Measure South Africa Mobile Report for 2017 indicates that Samsung remains the dominant mobile brand locally, with a 40 percent market share, while Apple is second at 10 percent, followed by Huawei at 9 percent. 
Globally, the operator said it commands a 10 percent market share for all smartphones, having shipped 153 million devices in 2017, up from 139 million in 2016. For premium devices, its market share is up to 8.4 percent, from less than 4 percent in 2016. Huawei GM for SA consumer business, Zhao Likun, noted that sales for the Mate 10 are up 66 percent from the Mate 9.
