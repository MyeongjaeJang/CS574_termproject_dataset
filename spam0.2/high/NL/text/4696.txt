Turkcell agrees new credit line, plans debt issue   09:31 CET | News 
The Capital Markets Board of Turkey approved Turkcell's application to issue debt of up to USD 750 million or its equivalent in another currency. These will be sold in international markets through private placement or sales to qualified investors abroad, without public offering. The debt instrument will be issued in one or more tranches and in various types and terms, Turkcell said in a press release. Fixed and floating interest rates will be determined as per the market conditions at the date of the issuance. 
In related news, Turkcell subsidiary Turkcell Finansman entered into a credit agreement with Bank of China for an amount of EUR 100 million. The maturity of the loan is 3.5 years and the annual interest rate is Euribor plus 1.25 percent.
