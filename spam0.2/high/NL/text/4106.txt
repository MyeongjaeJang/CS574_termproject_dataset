ICASA raises spectrum licence fees   09:44 CET | News 
South African telecoms regulator ICASA has announced price increases for spectrum licence fees and general licence fees, reports Mybroadband. Radio frequency spectrum licence fees and all administrative fees associated with applications and registrations relating to service licences will go up by 5.3 percent on 01 April. 
Its tariff increase is based on the consumer price index, said ICASA. This means the unit price per megahertz paired for radio frequency spectrum licences will increase from ZAR 2,226 to ZAR 2,344. Fees to apply for the amendment or transfer of service licences will increase from ZAR 57,873 to ZAR 60,940. Licence renewals will increase from ZAR 5,787 to ZAR 6,094.
