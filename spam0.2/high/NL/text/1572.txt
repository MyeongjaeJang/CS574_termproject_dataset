Lenovo launches S5, K5, and K5 Play phones in China Wednesday 21 March 2018 | 10:07 CET | News After over a year of not releasing any new phones, Lenovo has unveiled three phones in China on 20 March. The new models are S5 flagship phione, and two lower-end budget phones, namely the K5 and the K5 Play, presumably meant for children, The Verge reports. The Lenovo S5 features a 5.7-inch screen with an 18:9 aspect ratio and a 2160 x 1080 resolution. The device is powered by a Snapdragon 625 chip and a 3,000mAh battery. 
Customers who acquire the Lenovo S5 can opt for a 3GB or 4GB of RAM version, and 32GB, 64GB, or 128GB of storage. The S5 has dual 13-megapixel rear cameras and a 16-megapixel front camera. The device runs Android 8.0 Oreo out of the box with a ZUI skin overlay. The device is avaialbe for a starting price of CNY 999 (approximately USD 157.74) and goes up to CNY 1,499 (approximately 236.69). The S5 comes in midnight black and flame red colors. 
The K5 and K5 Play phones feature 5.7-inch displays with 1440 x 720 resolutions. The K5 has rear 13-megapixel and 5-megapixel dual cameras, while the K5 Play has 13-megapixel and 2-megapixel dual cameras. The K5 has a MediaTek MT6750 chip and runs Android Oreo, while the K5 Play has a Snapdragon 450 chip and runs Android Nougat. The K5 is on sale for CNY 899, while the K5 Play costs CNY 699.
